# Pehredaar — Strategy (v2: Discharge Defender + ClaimBack)

## 1. The thesis in one paragraph
India has a flood of *post-hoc bill checkers* (Jaanch, Evaakil, NYVO) and the government's own price apps — that lane is closed. But the moments where Indian families actually lose money are unprotected: the **discharge desk** (proportionate deduction wipes out lakhs the instant you sign) and the **claim rejection** (₹26,000 cr disallowed/yr, <1% appeal). Pehredaar owns those two moments. It is the only tool that intervenes *before the signature* and *fights the rejection after*. That is a defensible, viral, high-impact wedge.

## 2. Why now
- IRDAI tightening oversight; "proportionate deduction" is going viral on Reddit/Instagram as families discover the trap.
- 11% claim rejection rate; massive public anger at insurers post the global "deny, defend" discourse.
- AI bill/claim tools just proved demand (the ₹1.6cr→₹27L and US $195k→$33k stories) — but proved it for *generic chatbots*, leaving the *structured, India-specific, pre-signature* product open.
- Vision models (Sonnet 4.5) now read messy photographed bills well enough.

## 3. Competitive positioning
| Player | What they do | What they DON'T do (our gap) |
|---|---|---|
| Jaanch | post-hoc bill audit vs NPPA/CGHS/IRDAI | pre-signature; deduction prevention; claim appeal |
| Evaakil | bill check + room-rent deduction estimate | real-time at-desk; WhatsApp-native; appeals |
| NYVO | insurance guides + concierge | productized tool; instant analysis |
| Pharma Sahi Daam (govt) | medicine price + complaint | bills, deductions, claims |
| Claimable/Counterforce (US) | claim appeals | India / IRDAI / vernacular |
| ChatGPT | "upload your bill" | structure, citations, deduction math, escalation routing |

**Our one-line wedge:** *"We stop the hospital from taking your money before you sign — and we win it back if your claim is rejected."*

## 4. Moat (how we don't get cloned by Jaanch in a weekend)
1. **The pre-signature timing + WhatsApp flow** — operationally hard; nobody has the real-time at-desk loop.
2. **Curated policy sub-limit + IRDAI clause library** — the data asset compounds; appeal quality improves with every case.
3. **Vernacular + zero-app distribution** — reaches the tier-2/3 families who actually get trapped.
4. **Open-source the price-DB + rules engine** — credibility, grant fit (ACT/Omidyar/Mozilla), and a contributor flywheel. Keep the policy/clause library + UX as the edge.
5. **The growth loop** (see §6) — a shareable outcome card competitors lack.

## 5. Go-to-market
- **Phase 0 — design partners:** 20-30 real bills/rejections from network + Reddit (r/personalfinanceindia, r/india, r/HealthInsurance). Hand-verify outputs.
- **Phase 1 — WhatsApp soft launch:** single number, organic seeding in the exact Reddit/Insta threads where proportionate-deduction horror stories spread.
- **Phase 2 — creator loop:** the finance-influencer ecosystem already explains proportionate deduction; give them a tool to point at. Co-branded "Receipt of Justice" cards.
- **Phase 3 — B2B2C:** TPAs, employers, and patient-advocacy NGOs (ClaimBack as a white-label).

## 6. The growth loop (built-in virality)
Every resolved case generates an anonymized **"Receipt of Justice"** card: *"Pehredaar stopped a ₹82,000 proportionate deduction before signing"* / *"₹1.4L rejected claim → appeal drafted → reversed."* Shareable on WhatsApp/Insta with a one-tap "check yours." The pain is emotional and public → the share rate is high → CAC trends toward zero.

## 7. Monetization (later; free at the core for impact)
- **Free forever:** Discharge Defender basic check (the impact + acquisition engine).
- **Pay-what-you-want / small fee:** full ClaimBack appeal packet (US tools prove willingness-to-pay here).
- **B2B:** TPA/employer/NGO batch + white-label.
- **Never:** sell user data; never insurer-side (conflict of interest with the mission).

## 8. Impact framing (the mission bar)
North star = **rupees protected for ordinary families**. The poorest get hit hardest by deduction + denial. Free core + vernacular + WhatsApp = maximum reach. Open-source + grant-friendly = credibility and durability. This is the rare wedge where *max traction* and *max impact* point the same direction.

## 9. The honest risk to the thesis
If an incumbent (Jaanch/Evaakil) adds a deduction-calculator + appeal module before we get distribution, the window narrows. Our defense is **speed + distribution + the data/clause asset + open-source credibility**, not the feature itself. Ship the wedge fast, seed the loop, compound the library.
